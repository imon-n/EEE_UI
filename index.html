Frequently Asked Questions

What career opportunities are available for CU EEE graduates?

Graduates can work in power generation companies (BPDB, DESCO), telecommunication firms, automation industries, embedded systems, and software development. Many also pursue higher studies abroad.

Does the CU EEE department offer internship or research opportunities?

Yes, the CU EEE department provides internship and research opportunities in collaboration with industries, universities, and research centers. Students can participate in projects related to power systems, AI, IoT, and automation, and many get the chance to publish research papers in IEEE and other journals.

Are there any student clubs or activities in CU EEE?

Yes, CU EEE has student clubs like IEEE CU Student Branch, Robotics Club, and Optica CU Student Branch, which organize workshops, hackathons, and industrial tours to enhance learning.

What are the major subjects covered in the CU EEE curriculum?

The curriculum includes Power Systems, Electrical Machines, Control Systems, Electronics, Communication Engineering, Digital Signal Processing (DSP), Microcontrollers, and Renewable Energy.
